About Me

I'm a Master's student in Civil and Mineral Engineering at the University of Toronto, with a BScH in Environmental Life Sciences from Queen's University. I have a passion for sustainability, negative emission technologies, carbon capture, and machine learning optimization for environmental solutions. My academic and professional journey has focused on emissions mitigation strategies and the practical application of life cycle analysis, environmental assessment, and data science.

Projects

Key research projects include studies on soil organic matter quality in the High Arctic, phosphorus fractionation post-permafrost disturbances, and life cycle assessment of plastic alternatives. My work integrates environmental chemistry, climate modeling, and decision analysis frameworks.
